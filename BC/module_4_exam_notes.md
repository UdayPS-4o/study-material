# Module 4: Business Correspondence

## 1. Writing Skills in Business and Public Administration in India
In India, business correspondence combines traditional formalities with modern corporate efficiency. Given the linguistic diversity and hierarchy in Indian organizations (both public and private sectors), business writing demands politeness, clarity, and adherence to specific structural formats.

### Principles of Effective Writing
- **The 7 C's of Communication:** Clear, Concise, Concrete, Correct, Coherent, Complete, and Courteous.
- **Tone:** Professional, objective, and respectful.
- **Audience-Centric:** Tailoring the language based on who will be reading the document (internal vs. external stakeholders).
- **Structure:** Clear introduction, a coherent body containing the facts/details, and a concluding call to action.

## 2. Job Application
A formal letter or email sent to an employer to express interest in a specific job opening.
- **Structure:**
  - Sender’s Contact Information.
  - Date and Recipient’s Details.
  - Salutation (Dear Hiring Manager).
  - Introduction: State the position applied for and how you found out about it.
  - Body: Highlight relevant skills, experiences, and why you are a good fit for the company.
  - Conclusion: Express enthusiasm for an interview and provide contact details.
  - Sign-off (Sincerely, Your Name).

## 3. Bio-Data, Curriculum Vitae (CV), and Resume

- **Bio-Data (Biographical Data):** Traditionally used in India for government jobs, academic applications, or marriage proposals. It focuses heavily on personal details like date of birth, gender, religion, nationality, marital status, along with basic education and experience.
- **Resume:** Fast-paced, concise document (typically 1 to 2 pages). It highlights skills, work experience, and educational background specifically tailored to a specific job opening. Most common in the private corporate sector.
- **Curriculum Vitae (CV):** An in-depth, comprehensive document (often multiple pages) that covers academic background, research, publications, awards, affiliations, and detailed work history. Used primarily in academia, medicine, and research fields.

## 4. Notice
A written or printed announcement intended to inform a large number of people about an event, rule, or change.
- **Format:** Organization name at the top, the word "NOTICE" boldly, the date, an eye-catching heading, clear and concise body text detailing the Who/What/When/Where/Why, and the signature/designation of the issuer.

## 5. Agenda and Minutes of Meetings

- **Agenda:** An outline of the topics to be discussed during a meeting, distributed beforehand.
  - *Purpose:* Keeps the meeting organized, time-bound, and ensures participants arrive prepared.
  - *Components:* Date, time, location, list of topics, and designated speakers/time limits for each point.
- **Minutes of Meetings (MoM):** The official written record of a meeting's proceedings.
  - *Purpose:* Provides a historical record, documents decisions made, and tracks action items.
  - *Components:* Attendees and absentees, approval of previous minutes, summary of discussions, decisions taken, and action items with deadlines and responsible persons.

## 6. Memorandum (Memo)
An internal written document used for establishing policy, clarifying procedures, or delivering brief announcements among employees within the same organization.
- **Format:**
  - **TO:** (Recipients)
  - **FROM:** (Sender)
  - **DATE:**
  - **SUBJECT:** (Brief description of the memo’s purpose)
- **Characteristics:** Direct, lacks formal opening/closing salutations, informational, and usually ends with a call to action or a summary.
