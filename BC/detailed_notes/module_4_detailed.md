# Module 4: Business Correspondence (Detailed Exam Notes)

## 1. Writing Skills in Business and Public Administration
Public administration (government) and corporate business both rely entirely on clear correspondence. Bad writing costs companies money through errors and inefficiencies.
**The "7 Cs of Effective Communication" (Crucial for Long Answers):**
1. **Clear:** Use simple language. Don't use "utilize" when you can use "use". Ensure the core message is unmistakable.
2. **Concise:** Avoid wordiness. Respect the reader’s time. Stick to necessary facts.
3. **Concrete:** Be specific. Instead of "sales are up," say "sales increased by 15% in Q3."
4. **Correct:** Zero grammatical errors and historically/factually accurate data.
5. **Considerate (Audience-centric):** Write from the "You" perspective, focusing on the reader's benefits and viewpoints.
6. **Complete:** Provide all facts necessary for the reader to take action. Who, what, when, where, why, and how.
7. **Courteous:** Maintain a polite, professional tone, even when writing a complaint.

## 2. Job Application Documents
A job application initiates an individual's relationship with a company.
- **Cover Letter:** A customized letter accompanying a resume. It should NOT just repeat the resume. It should tell a narrative—why you are passionate about the company and how a specific past achievement aligns perfectly with the role.

## 3. Differentiating Bio-Data, CV, and Resume
*(Use this table structure in exams for easy reading and high marks)*

| Feature | Bio-Data (Biographical Data) | Resume (French for 'Summary') | CV (Curriculum Vitae - 'Course of Life') |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Length** | 1-3 Pages | 1-2 Pages | 2 to 10+ Pages |
| **Focus** | Personal attributes (Age, religion, marital status, health) + basic education. | Heavily focused on skills, achievements, and work experience tailored to a specific job. | Extremely comprehensive academic history, research, publications, awards. |
| **Usage** | Traditional Indian government jobs, marriage proposals. | Private sector corporate jobs (IT, Finance, Marketing). | Academic, scientific, research, and medical fellowships. |
| **Customization** | Generally static. | Highly customized for every single job application. | Static list of all accomplishments; cover letter is what changes. |

## 4. Notice
A notice is a formal announcement displayed publicly targeting a specific group.
- **Structure:**
  1. Name of Issuing Authority/Organization (Top Center).
  2. The word "NOTICE" in uppercase capitals.
  3. Date of issuance (Left-aligned).
  4. Descriptive Headline/Subject.
  5. Body: Must answer the 5 Ws - What is the event? When is it? Where is it? Who is invited? Whom to contact for info?
  6. Signature, Name, and Designation of Issuing Person.

## 5. Agenda and Minutes of Meetings (MoM)
Meetings without structure waste massive corporate resources.
- **Agenda:** Dispatched 2-3 days *before* the meeting.
  - Forms a roadmap.
  - Lists the Date, Time, Venue.
  - Lists sequential agenda items with time allotments (e.g., 10:00 AM - Budget Review (15 mins) - led by John).
- **Minutes of Meetings (MoM):** Sent quickly *after* the meeting.
  - An official, legally binding historical record of the meeting.
  - *Contents:* List of attendees, list of absentees.
  - *Key Element:* The Action Plan—who is doing what, and by what specific deadline (e.g., "Action: Sarah to submit marketing draft by Friday EOD").

## 6. Memorandum (Memo)
A memo is an internal intra-office communication. It is less formal than a letter but more formal than an email. Used to announce policy changes or widespread operational updates.
- **Structure (Top Header Block is crucial to memorize):**
  - **TO:** All Department Heads
  - **FROM:** The CEO
  - **DATE:** October 24, 2024
  - **SUBJECT:** Mandatory Cybersecurity Training
- **Body:** Direct and to the point. No "Dear Sir" or "Yours faithfully" required. Gets straight to the facts and required actions.
