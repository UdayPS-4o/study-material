## 1. WRITING SKILLS IN BUSINESS AND PUBLIC ADMINISTRATION

### 1.1 Importance of Written Communication in Business & Public Administration
Written communication is the backbone of professional interaction. In India, both the private sector and public administration (government offices, PSUs) rely heavily on formal written documents for:
- Creating permanent, legally valid records
- Establishing accountability and responsibility
- Communicating policies, decisions, and grievances
- Ensuring transparency and traceability

### 1.2 Characteristics of Effective Business Writing (The 7 Cs)
| Principle | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Clarity** | Easy to understand; no ambiguity | Use simple sentences; avoid jargon |
| **Conciseness** | Say more with fewer words | Avoid redundant phrases: "at this point in time" → "now" |
| **Correctness** | Grammar, spelling, facts are accurate | Proofread thoroughly |
| **Completeness** | All required information included | Who, What, When, Where, Why, How |
| **Concreteness** | Specific and vivid | "Sales increased 20% in Q1" not "Sales went up" |
| **Consideration** | Written from reader's perspective (You-attitude) | "You will receive your order by Friday" |
| **Courtesy** | Polite, respectful tone | "We regret any inconvenience caused" |

### 1.3 Writing Process (POWER Method)
1. **P**lan — Identify purpose, audience, and key message
2. **O**rganize — Arrange ideas logically (chronological, order of importance, problem-solution)
3. **W**rite — Draft the content, focus on content not perfection
4. **E**valuate — Review for clarity, completeness, and tone
5. **R**evise — Edit grammar, structure, and language

## 2. EFFECTIVE WRITING

### 2.1 Principles of Effective Writing
1. **Know your purpose:** Inform, persuade, request, or respond?
2. **Know your audience:** Senior officer, client, colleague, general public?
3. **Structure properly:** Introduction → Body → Conclusion
4. **Use active voice:** "The committee approved the proposal" (not "The proposal was approved by the committee")
5. **Use short sentences and paragraphs:** Each paragraph = one idea
6. **Avoid jargon and clichés:** Unless audience-specific terminology is needed
7. **Be objective:** Especially in business reports and public admin documents
8. **Use appropriate tone:** Formal for official communication; professional-friendly for clients

### 2.2 Common Pitfalls in Business Writing
| Problem | Example | Correction |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Wordiness | "Due to the fact that" | "Because" |
| Passive voice overuse | "Errors were made" | "We made errors" |
| Jargon | "Synergize our deliverables" | "Work together on our outputs" |
| Ambiguity | "The project will be done soon" | "The project will be completed by May 30" |
| Tautology | "Free gift," "past history" | "Gift," "History" |
| Nominalization | "Make a decision" | "Decide" |

## 3. JOB APPLICATION

### 3.1 What Is a Job Application Letter?
A job application (cover letter) is a formal letter **accompanying a resume/CV** that introduces the applicant, highlights relevant skills, and expresses interest in a specific position.

### 3.2 Purpose and Importance
- First point of contact with a potential employer
- Personalizes and complements the resume
- Demonstrates written communication skills
- Persuades the reader to review the attached resume
- Specifically addresses how the applicant fits the job requirements

### 3.3 Types of Application Letters
1. **Solicited Application:** Response to an advertised job vacancy
2. **Unsolicited Application (Prospecting):** Sending application even when no vacancy is announced
3. **Referral Letter:** Mentioning a mutual contact who referred you

### 3.4 Structure of a Job Application Letter

```
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[City, State, PIN]
[Phone Number]
[Email Address]
[Date]

[Hiring Manager's Name]
[Designation]
[Company Name]
[Company Address]

Subject: Application for the Post of [Position Name]

Dear Mr./Ms. [Last Name],

PARAGRAPH 1 — OPENING (How you heard about the job; which position; brief statement of interest)
I am writing to apply for the position of [Job Title] as advertised in [Source] on [Date]. With [X years] of experience in [field] and a strong background in [key skills], I am confident in my ability to contribute effectively to your organization.

PARAGRAPH 2 — BODY (Why you are qualified; match your skills to job requirements)
During my tenure at [Previous Company], I was responsible for [key responsibilities], which enabled me to develop expertise in [relevant skills]. I have also [notable achievement with measurable result]. My [specific skill] aligns directly with the requirements outlined in your job posting.

PARAGRAPH 3 — CLOSING (Call to action; request for interview; thank you)
I have enclosed my resume for your review and would welcome the opportunity to discuss my qualifications in an interview at your convenience. Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to hearing from you.

Yours sincerely,
[Signature]
[Your Name]

Enclosures: Resume, Certificates (as applicable)
```

### 3.5 Tips for a Strong Application Letter
- Customize every letter for the specific job and company
- Use specific examples and measurable achievements
- Match keywords from the job description
- Keep it to one page
- Use professional email address
- Error-free grammar and spelling
- Confident but not arrogant tone

## 4. BIO DATA

### 4.1 What Is a Bio Data?
Bio data (**Biographical Data**) is the **oldest form of career document** used in India. It provides **basic personal information** about an individual, primarily focusing on:
- Personal details (name, date of birth, religion, nationality, marital status)
- Educational qualifications
- Work experience
- Contact details
- References

### 4.2 Uses of Bio Data
- Used in **government job applications**, **marriage proposals** (common in South Asia), and **matrimonial matching**
- Rarely used in modern corporate recruitment (replaced by CV/Resume)
- Required for certain **government/PSU forms and applications**

### 4.3 Format of a Bio Data

```
BIO DATA

Name                  : [Full Name]
Father's Name         : [Father's Name]
Date of Birth         : [DD/MM/YYYY]
Age                   : [X years]
Gender                : [Male/Female/Other]
Nationality           : Indian
Religion              : [Religion]
Marital Status        : [Single/Married]
Languages Known       : [Languages]

Permanent Address     : [Full Address]
Contact Number        : [Phone Number]
Email ID              : [Email Address]

EDUCATIONAL QUALIFICATIONS:
[Table with Qualification | Institution | Year | Percentage]

WORK EXPERIENCE:
[Organization | Designation | Duration | Responsibilities]

SKILLS               : [List of relevant skills]

DECLARATION:
I hereby declare that all the information provided above is true and correct to the best of my knowledge.

Date:
Place:
                                           Signature
                                           [Name]
```

### 4.4 Bio Data vs. Resume vs. CV
| Feature | Bio Data | Resume | CV |
|---------|----------|--------|-----|
| **Focus** | Personal info | Professional skills & experience | Academic/research profile |
| **Length** | 1 page | 1-2 pages | 2+ pages (no limit) |
| **Use** | Government jobs, matrimonial | Corporate jobs | Academic institutions, research |
| **Customization** | Fixed format | Tailored per job | Detailed, consistent |
| **Personal Info** | Extensive | Minimal/none | Moderate |
| **Popularity** | Declining | Most used globally | Used in academia |

## 5. CURRICULUM VITAE (CV)

### 5.1 Definition
CV (Latin: *Curriculum Vitae* = "Course of Life") is a **comprehensive, detailed document** that covers the entirety of a person's academic and professional history — primarily used in **academic, research, and international job applications**.

### 5.2 Structure of a CV

```
CURRICULUM VITAE
[Full Name] | [Contact] | [LinkedIn Profile] | [Email]

PERSONAL PROFILE (Optional):
[2-3 sentences summarizing your professional identity]

EDUCATION:
[Reverse chronological order]
- PhD/Masters/Bachelors: Institution, Year, Grade/CGPA
- Thesis/Dissertation Title (for research positions)

RESEARCH EXPERIENCE:
[Detailed description of research projects]

PUBLICATIONS:
[Journal articles, conference papers, books — APA/MLA format]

PRESENTATIONS/CONFERENCES:
[Events where work was presented]

AWARDS & HONORS:

PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE:
[Positions held, organizations, dates, responsibilities]

SKILLS:
[Technical, software, languages]

PROFESSIONAL MEMBERSHIPS:

REFERENCES:
[2-3 references with their designation, institution, email]
```

### 5.3 Key Features of a Good CV
- Comprehensive (typically 3-10 pages for experienced academics)
- No lies or exaggerations
- Publications formatted in proper citation style
- Updated regularly
- Academic tone throughout

## 6. RESUME

### 6.1 Definition and Purpose
A resume is a **brief, targeted, curated document** (1-2 pages) highlighting an applicant's most relevant work experience, education, and skills for a **specific job position**.

### 6.2 Types of Resumes

| Type | Description | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Chronological** | Work experience in reverse time order | Consistent career progression |
| **Functional** | Skills and functions highlighted over dates | Career changers, employment gaps |
| **Combination/Hybrid** | Mix of chronological and functional | Showcasing skills + experience |
| **Targeted** | Fully customized for one specific job | High-stakes applications |
| **Infographic/Visual** | Graphic design-heavy visual resume | Creative industries |
| **ATS-Optimized** | Keyword-rich for Applicant Tracking Systems | Large companies with automated screening |

### 6.3 Standard Resume Format

```
[FULL NAME]
[Phone] | [Email] | [LinkedIn] | [City, State]

PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY:
Dynamic and results-oriented [Job Title] with [X years] of experience in [industry]. Proven track record of [key achievement]. Seeking to leverage expertise in [skills] to contribute to [Company].

SKILLS:
• [Skill 1]      • [Skill 2]      • [Skill 3]
• [Skill 4]      • [Skill 5]      • [Skill 6]

PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE:
[Most Recent Company] | [City]                    [Month Year – Month Year]
[Job Title]
• Achieved [result] by [action] using [tool/skill]
• Led a team of [X] to [accomplishment] — resulted in [measurable outcome]

[Previous Company] | [City]                       [Month Year – Month Year]
[Job Title]
• [Bullet points of key responsibilities and achievements]

EDUCATION:
[Degree], [Major] | [University Name]              [Year]
GPA: [X.XX] (include only if > 3.5/4.0)

CERTIFICATIONS:
• [Certification Name] – [Issuing Body] (Year)

PROJECTS (Optional):
• [Project Name]: [Brief description + outcome/impact]
```

### 6.4 Resume Writing Tips
- Tailor resume for EACH job application
- Use **action verbs**: Achieved, Managed, Developed, Led, Designed, Implemented
- Quantify achievements: "Increased sales by 35% in Q2 2023"
- Keep to 1 page (fresher) or 2 pages (experienced)
- Use professional font (Calibri, Arial, Georgia) at 10-12pt
- Use bullet points, not paragraphs
- Include white space for readability
- **Do NOT include:** photo, age, religion, marital status (in most global contexts), salary expectations

## 7. NOTICE

### 7.1 What Is a Notice?
A notice is a **formal written announcement** communicating important information to a group of people. It informs them of meetings, events, examinations, appointments, rules, or urgent matters.

### 7.2 Types of Notices
- **Meeting Notice:** Inform about an upcoming meeting
- **Public Notice:** Addressed to the general public (in newspapers/public boards)
- **Office Notice:** Internal communication to staff and employees
- **Legal Notice:** Formal demand or warning with legal implications

### 7.3 Format of a Notice

```
[Name of Organization/Institution]
NOTICE
Date: [DD/MM/YYYY]

[Title/Subject of Notice — written in bold or underline]

This is to inform all [concerned persons — members/students/employees/etc.] that [body of the notice — what, when, where, who it affects, actions required].

[Closing statement if any — RSVP, penalties for non-compliance, etc.]

[Signature]
[Name of Issuing Authority]
[Designation]
[Contact Details]
```

### 7.4 Example Notice

```
DELHI PUBLIC SCHOOL, ROHINI
NOTICE
Date: 12 April 2026

ANNUAL SPORTS DAY

This is to inform all students that the Annual Sports Day will be held on Saturday, April 25, 2026, on the school grounds from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM. All students participating in events are required to report by 8:30 AM in their respective track suits. Students are requested to confirm their participation with their class teachers by April 15, 2026.

Principal
Delhi Public School, Rohini
Contact: 011-XXXXXXXX
```

### 7.5 Characteristics of a Good Notice
- **Brevity:** Short and to the point
- **Clarity:** No ambiguity about who, what, when, where
- **Prominence:** Clearly displayed or titled
- **Authority:** Signed by an authorized person
- **Completeness:** Contains all necessary information

## 8. AGENDA AND MINUTES OF MEETINGS

### 8.1 AGENDA

#### What Is an Agenda?
An agenda is a **pre-meeting document** that outlines the **topics to be discussed** during a meeting, in the order they will be taken up. It is circulated to participants before the meeting.

#### Purposes of an Agenda:
- Informs participants what to prepare
- Keeps the meeting focused and on-track
- Ensures no important topic is missed
- Respects participants' time

#### Components of an Agenda:
1. Name of the organization/committee
2. Type and number of the meeting
3. Date, time, and venue
4. List of agenda items (numbered)
5. Name of the convener/organizer

#### Format of an Agenda:

```
[ORGANIZATION NAME]
AGENDA

Meeting Type: [Ordinary/Extraordinary/Annual General Meeting]
Meeting No.: [Number]
Date & Time: [DD/MM/YYYY at HH:MM AM/PM]
Venue: [Location]

AGENDA ITEMS:
1. Welcome and call to order
2. Confirmation of quorum
3. Reading and confirmation of the minutes of the previous meeting
4. Matters arising from the previous minutes
5. [Main Agenda Item 1 — specific subject]
6. [Main Agenda Item 2]
7. [Main Agenda Item 3]
8. Any Other Business (AOB)
9. Date, time, and venue of next meeting
10. Vote of thanks and adjournment

[Signature]
[Name of Secretary/Convener]
[Designation]
```

---

### 8.2 MINUTES OF MEETINGS

#### What Are Minutes?
Minutes are the **official, permanent written record of proceedings** of a meeting — including discussions, decisions made, and action items assigned.

#### Types of Minutes:
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Resolution Minutes** | Only records decisions/resolutions passed |
| **Narrative Minutes** | Records full discussion and decision |
| **Action Minutes** | Records what was decided and who is responsible for each action |

#### Key Elements of Minutes:
1. Name of the organization
2. Type of meeting and number
3. Date, time, venue
4. Members present, absent, and guests
5. Apologies received
6. Confirmation of previous minutes
7. Proceedings — discussion and decisions on each agenda item
8. Action items (who, what, by when)
9. Date of next meeting
10. Signature of chairperson and secretary

#### Format of Minutes:

```
[ORGANIZATION NAME]
MINUTES OF MEETING

Meeting Type: [Type]       Meeting No.: [Number]
Date & Time: [DD/MM/YYYY, HH:MM]
Venue: [Location]

Members Present:
1. [Name, Designation] — Chairperson
2. [Name, Designation]
...

Apologies Received: [Names of members who sent apologies]

Members Absent: [Names]

---

1. CALL TO ORDER
   The Chairperson called the meeting to order at [time] and welcomed all members.

2. CONFIRMATION OF QUORUM
   The Secretary confirmed that a quorum of [X] members was present.

3. CONFIRMATION OF PREVIOUS MINUTES
   The minutes of the previous meeting held on [date] were read out and confirmed as a true record. Moved by: [Name]. Seconded by: [Name].

4. AGENDA ITEM 1: [Title]
   Discussion: [Summary of discussion]
   Decision/Resolution: [What was decided]
   Action: [Who is responsible, by when]

5. AGENDA ITEM 2: [Title]
   Discussion: [Summary]
   Decision: [Resolution]
   Action: [Action item]

6. ANY OTHER BUSINESS
   [Discussion if any]

7. NEXT MEETING
   The next meeting will be held on [date] at [time] at [venue].

8. ADJOURNMENT
   The Chairperson declared the meeting adjourned at [time] with a vote of thanks.

Confirmed as a true and accurate record:

[Signature]                          [Signature]
Chairperson                          Secretary
Date: ___________                    Date: ___________
```

#### Qualities of Good Minutes:
- **Accuracy:** Faithful record of what was discussed and decided
- **Impartiality:** No opinions; neutral, objective language
- **Clarity:** Simple, clear language; no ambiguity
- **Completeness:** Covers all agenda items
- **Brevity:** Concise but not incomplete
- **Timeliness:** Circulated promptly after the meeting

## 9. MEMORANDUM (MEMO)

### 9.1 What Is a Memorandum?
A memorandum (plural: memoranda) or **memo** is a **short, internal written communication** used within an organization to convey information, instructions, policies, or reminders to employees or departments.

**Origin:** From the Latin *memorandum* — "a thing to be remembered."

### 9.2 Characteristics of a Memo
- Internal communication only (within the organization)
- No formal salutation ("Dear Sir/Madam") needed
- Brief, direct, and focused on a single subject
- Does not require a complimentary close ("Yours sincerely")
- Uses a standardized format

### 9.3 Types of Memos
| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Informational Memo** | Share updates, news, or policy changes |
| **Directive Memo** | Give orders/instructions to employees |
| **Request Memo** | Ask for information, approval, or resources |
| **Response Memo** | Reply to a previous memo or query |
| **Confirmatory Memo** | Confirm a verbal agreement or decision |
| **Report Memo** | Submit a brief internal report |

### 9.4 Format of a Memo

```
[ORGANIZATION LETTERHEAD]

MEMORANDUM

To       : [Name(s) / Department(s)]
From     : [Sender's Name and Designation]
Date     : [DD/MM/YYYY]
Subject  : [Clear, concise subject line]

BODY:

OPENING PARAGRAPH — State the purpose of the memo in the first sentence.
[Purpose of memo — what this is about and why]

BODY PARAGRAPHS — Provide all necessary details.
[Details, background, supporting information, policies, data, etc.]

CLOSING PARAGRAPH — State what action is required or expected, and by when.
[Action required — who needs to do what, by when]

[Signature or initials of sender]
[Name]
[Designation]

CC: [Others who should receive a copy, if any]
Attachments: [If any]
```

### 9.5 Memo vs. Email vs. Letter

| Aspect | Memo | Email | Letter |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| **Direction** | Internal | Internal & External | Usually External |
| **Format** | Standardized | Flexible | Formal structured |
| **Salutation** | Not required | Required | Required |
| **Closing** | Not required | Required | Required |
| **Record** | Physical/digital | Digital | Physical/digital |
| **Speed** | Moderate | Fastest | Slowest |
| **Length** | Short-medium | Short | Any length |

### 9.6 Sample Memo

```
APEX MANUFACTURING PVT. LTD.

MEMORANDUM

To      : All Department Heads
From    : Priya Sharma, HR Manager
Date    : 12 April 2026
Subject : Mandatory Safety Training — April 20, 2026

This is to inform all department heads that a mandatory safety training session will be conducted on April 20, 2026, from 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM in Conference Hall B. All employees under your supervision are required to attend this session without fail.

Please ensure that attendance registers are submitted to the HR Department by April 19, 2026. Employees who are unable to attend must complete the online module by April 25, 2026.

Your cooperation in ensuring 100% participation is requested.

Priya Sharma
HR Manager

CC: General Manager, Operations Director
```
